What is the Opportunity Card?

The Opportunity Card (Chancenkarte) is Germany's new residence permit under the Skilled Immigration Act (¬ß20a AufenthG). It represents a paradigm shift in German immigration policy.

Unlike traditional visas, it allows qualified professionals from countries like India to enter Germany without a job offer and look for employment for up to one year.

Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers to the most common questions about Germany‚Äôs Opportunity Card.

What is the total cost for the Opportunity Card?

There are two main costs: (1) the visa application fee (approx. ‚Ç¨75) and (2) the mandatory proof of funds, which is a Blocked Account of approx. ‚Ç¨11,904 for the year. This money is still yours; it just proves you can support yourself.

Can I bring my family (spouse/children) with me on the Opportunity Card?

No, the Opportunity Card is for the primary applicant only. However, once you find a qualified job and convert your visa to a work permit, you can immediately apply for a family reunion visa.

What kind of job do I need to find in Germany?

To convert your visa, you must find ‚Äúqualified employment.‚Äù This generally means a full-time job that matches your university degree or vocational training and meets a certain salary level.

What if I don't find a job in one year?

The Opportunity Card is issued for one year to find a job. If you have a binding job offer, it can be extended for up to two more years, giving you ample time.
